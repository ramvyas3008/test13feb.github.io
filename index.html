  • Lata Mangeshkar recorded songs in over thirty-six Indian languages and a few foreign languages, though primarily in Hindi, Bengali, Marathi.[9] She also sang in English, Russian, Dutch, and even Swahili.
  • In 1942, when Mangeshkar was 13, her father died of heart disease.
  • She sang the song "Naachu Yaa Gade, Khelu Saari Mani Haus Bhaari", which was composed by Sadashivrao Nevrekar for Vasant Joglekar's Marathi movie Kiti Hasaal (1942), but the song was dropped from the final cut.
  • She moved to Mumbai in 1945 when Master Vinayak's company moved its headquarters there.
  • After Vinayak's death in 1948, music director Ghulam Haider mentored her as a singer.
